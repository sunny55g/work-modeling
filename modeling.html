OVERVIEW

The fashion and cosmetics industries are highly consumer-centric, impacting nearly everyone worldwide. These industries consume vast quantities of raw materials and can significantly harm the environment. In the face of escalating plastic pollution and the pressing need for environmental conservation, our project, PETAL , presents a novel solution by seamlessly integrating plastic waste management with the preservation of Indigenous sandalwood trees through the power of synthetic biology. Here, we will employ engineered microorganism to uptake plastic (PET) monomers and then utilize them to produce sandalwood oil.

Flux Balance Analysis+

Introduction:

The need of a computational model: We are in an age where experimental Data required to solve certain problems are already available, A lot of interactomes are studied, and the data is ever-increasing; the simplest of mathematical or computational models uses some of these available datasets and applies mathematical relations between them (most of the time correlated to the experimental data available) and predicts the outcomes if some of the parameters were changed. It helps us predict what outcomes we would get from an experiment. It thus helps us design it, and also reduces the number of experiments and test extremities, which are not safe but probably possible just by changing parameters and their relations. The mathematical model we would be working with is an interactome of metabolites in a biological organism known as GSSM (Genome-scale metabolic model).

The Need for a Metabolic Model

Our idea was to metabolically engineer Pseudomonas putida to grow solely on the monomers of PET, namely EG (Ethylene Glycol) and Terephthalic acid, and produce sesquiterpenoids like α-santol, β-santalol, α-exo-bergamotol and epi-β-santol as secondary metabolites. For that genes were obtained or designed to add a few metabolic pathways required to obtain the target metabolite. Thus, a GSSM was required to predict the changes in the metabolic network.

What is a GSSM?

A Genome-Scale Metabolic model is a set of inter-dependent linear equations, where the equations represent reactions (assumed to be at their steady state) catalyzed by proteins obtained from the genomic information of the organism. They all have a default objective of ‘Biomass’, which is a merged form of various equations(reactions) and elements(metabolites) required for the model (organism) to survive in an assumed live state backed up by actual wet lab experiments and metabolomics study of the organism.

Objectives of our model:

  • To be able to take up Ethylene Glycol and Terephthalic acid and produce santalol and isomers.
  • Predict the probable metabolic path taken by our metabolites
  • Predict the genes to knock or overexpress to get a better yield of our product.

Our approach:

Background and selection of model:

Making a GSSM is has become trivial due to the vast availability of data and well-designed pipelines, but validating a model and checking how closely it resembles the biological system is requires time and experimental validation. Trivia on models:

  • Model iJP815: This GSSM was developed by Puchalaka et al 2008, It was one of the early attempts to to make a GSSM of p.putida KT2440 to address it’s metabolic wiring as well as biocatalysis. The model was validated by making various knockout variants of the bacterium and high throughput data analysis. The Auxotrophy was correctly predicted by the modelmin 75% of the cases.
  • Model PpuQY1140: The GSSM was developed for Pseudomonas putida KT2440 by Qianqian.et.al 2017 . They Compared four previously published Specifically, iJN746 (a precursor of iJN1463), iJP815, iJP962(a later version of iJP815) and MBEL1071. Further, the discrepancies between the models leading to inconsistent pathway calculation results were identified. The group also corrected the models based on literature to ensure all the calculated Synthesis and uptake pathways are similar further built the pathway consensus model(PpuQY1140), and updated it to the latest genome annotation available while publishing. Hence the GSSM had stoichiometrically correct reactions but no Formula or charge information on the metabolites.
  • Model iJN1463: Built by Nogales et.al 2020, This new GSSM incorporated several hundred additional genes and associated reactions and new nutrients supporting growth. This model was validated experimentally by in vivo growth screens on previously untested carbon and nitrogen source. The Knockout predictions of essential genes was also very accurate. This model considers some metabolites to get accumulate in the bacterium ans treats them as sinks, Also This is the only GSSM to have accounted for the formula and charge of the metabolites and thus The carbon flux (c-flux) could also be tested.
    The Models will be compared to each other later on the basis of how accurately the predicted metabolic pathway opted by Etlylene Glycol snf Terepthalic acid coinsides with the possible patheay predicted via experimental methods.

    • FBA(Flux Balance Analysis)

    This is one of the core methods to analyze a GSMM, In a Flux balance analysis, we set a Reaction of interest as our objective, and then the linear programming algorithms of COBRApy, like GLPK(GNU Linear Programming Kit) solve the matrix of linear equations to provide maximum value to the objective reaction. Generally, the Default objective of a GSSM is to solve for Biomass, which represents the growth of our bacteria.
    For our model to be able to grow on EG and TPA, we add them as new metabolites and connect them to existing metabolites via introducing their respective uptake and assimilation reactions. Following thisNext, to make our model grow solely on EG and TPA, we set the uptakes of turned off all other carbon sources in the model to zero uptakes and made it a feasible solution for biomass to grow on TPA and EG. Further, our target metabolitesTo Make it produce the Target Metabolite, we added Santalene and Santalol, were added as new metabolites and their corresponding enzymatic added necessary reactions to synthesise them were connected with the exisiting MEP pathway reactions. them with existing metabolites and secretion of santalol.

    The Assumptions of the Analysis are:
  • The fluxes of the entire model are calculated assuming that reactions and their individual fluxes have achieved steady state, making the stochiometric matrix easy to solver by forming linear equations.
  • Each reaction represents the function of an enzyme or an import-export system.
  • TTerepthalic acid and ethylene glycol were given as uptake reactions from the extracellular environment. The uptake values were initially set as per model’s preexisting uptake bounds for other metabolites.
  • Santalol synthesised as part of the model’s reactions would be secreted into the extracellular environment, acting as a carbon sink.
  • The Biomass reaction is a an accumulated endpoint reaction of essential metabolites, that were determined from the models listed above (Put refs. The biomass reaction flux acts as a proxy for growtth and is the condition for organism survival in silicoThe following reactions and metabolites were added to each of the models.

    Modifications to the GSSMs:
  • The above mentioned reactions and metabolites were added to each of the models. With The exception of PpuQY1140 in which the Glyoxalate Shunt catalyzed by malate synthase was borrowed from iJN1463 and added.
  • The Default upper and lower bounds of the reactions were changed from to -999999 to 999999 to a more feasible -1000 to 1000

    • Simulating Growth coupled production of Santalol:

    In an ideal biological scenario, if the organism produces a secondary metabolite in excess its growth gets sacrificed, To mimic this in our model we had set two objectives:

  • Santalol_secretion : To obtain our product.
  • Biomass: To mimic Organism survival and growth

    • The objectives were solved for minimum and maximum fluxes through them and the The Data was plotted in form of a production Envelope using the ‘plot_flux_space’ function from Strain design Package(Schneider et.al.2022). The function gives a Graphical output of the flux space and and array of the points used for building the flux space. The x axis represents the Biomass as an objective and The y axis represents Santalol secretion as an objective. After selecting the the desired flux of santalol secretion paired with the highest possible Biomass flux at that condition, A new objective was set to model:
    new_objective = 1.0 Biomass + 1.0 Santalol_secretion
    Where the upper bound of the objectives corresponds to to the X and Y coordinates of the chosen point in the Production envelope plot built with strain design. The new objective states to the solver of the model to give equal priorities to both Biomass and Santalol_secretion.


    Diverting Flux towards Santalol production pathway:

    To find which genes to upregulate in Pseudomonas putida. we first kept the objective as Biomass, At this point it mimicked its Laboratory counter part which can grow solely on EG and TPA (generously provided by Dr. Oliver Brandenberg). For The bacteria to Grow and Produce Santalol we change the objective to “new_objective” .Then we noted the Change of flux and pathways in the model and observed a branching point in utiliztion of Pyruvate. The reaction catalyzed by Deoxy xylulose-5-phosphate synthase(DXS) which Catalyzed the following reaction:
    Pyruvate + Glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate -> 1-Deoxy xylulose-5-phosphate
    was inactive when Biomass was the objective got activated when santalol secretion was set as a co-objective. This reaction Leads to the MEP pathway and helps in production of santalol, as observed from the Flux Mapping of all the three modified GSSMs. This observation from the Flux Maps and backed up by Dr.jordi (paper required) we Decided to upregulate DXS.
